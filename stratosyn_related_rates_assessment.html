// SCENARIO

A conical reservoir (vertex pointing down) has a height of 12 feet and a radius of 4 feet at the top. Water is draining from the bottom at a constant rate of 2 cubic feet per minute. At the moment when the water depth is 6 feet, determine how fast the water level is falling.

Given: dV/dt = −2 ft³/min (volume decreasing)
Cone: H = 12 ft, R = 4 ft (full dimensions)
Find: dh/dt when h = 6 ft

HINT → dV/dt is given (volume rate). dh/dt is unknown (height rate). h = 6 ft is the specific instant. The cone dimensions (H = 12, R = 4) are fixed constants — they do not change with time.
02
Build the Volume Equation
// Similar triangles to eliminate r
LOCKED

The volume of a cone is V = (1/3)πr²h. The problem: we have two unknowns (r and h) but only one equation. Use the similar triangles relationship from the cone's geometry to write r in terms of h, then substitute.

// SIMILAR TRIANGLES: Write r in terms of h
// SUBSTITUTED VOLUME FORMULA (one variable only)
HINT → Full cone: R/H = 4/12 = 1/3. So at any water height h, the water radius r = h/3. Substitute: V = (1/3)π(h/3)²h = (1/3)π(h²/9)h = πh³/27. Final equation: V = (π/27)h³
03
Differentiate with Respect to Time
// Chain rule — implicit differentiation
LOCKED

Now differentiate both sides of V = (π/27)h³ with respect to time t. Remember: both V and h are functions of t, so you must use the chain rule. Show the derivative before plugging in any numbers.

// dV/dt in terms of dh/dt (before substituting values)
HINT → Differentiate V = (π/27)h³ with respect to t. dV/dt = (π/27) · 3h² · (dh/dt) dV/dt = (π/9)h² · (dh/dt) The chain rule gives you the extra factor of dh/dt because h is a function of t.
04
Substitute & Solve for dh/dt
// Plug in h = 6, dV/dt = −2
LOCKED

You have: dV/dt = (π/9)h²(dh/dt). Now substitute the known values (dV/dt = −2, h = 6) and solve algebraically for dh/dt. Show your algebra — do not skip steps.

// SUBSTITUTION STEP
// SOLVE: dh/dt = ?
HINT → −2 = (π/9)(6²)(dh/dt) −2 = (π/9)(36)(dh/dt) −2 = 4π(dh/dt) dh/dt = −2/(4π) = −1/(2π) ft/min The negative sign confirms the water level is FALLING.

This extended response task was selected because it demands conceptual layering rather than procedural recall — a student cannot pattern-match their way through related rates without genuinely understanding what each derivative means in context. Aligned to AP Calculus AB standard CHA-3.D and Standards for Mathematical Practice 1, 4, and 6, the task requires students to model a physical system, connect geometric constraints through similar triangles, apply implicit differentiation, and communicate their result with precision and meaning. The sequential, phase-based structure mirrors the cognitive demands of authentic engineering work, making the math feel purposeful rather than arbitrary. To promote equity and access, the embedded diagram reduces language load for ELL students while still requiring full written interpretation; the hint system provides scaffolding without removing challenge, supporting IEP and 504 students who may need processing support without altering the rigor of the standard. The analytic rubric communicates expectations at four distinct performance levels across all five conceptual dimensions, ensuring that a student who demonstrates strong reasoning but makes a single arithmetic error is evaluated fairly and given actionable feedback toward mastery.
